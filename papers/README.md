# Downloaded Papers

This file indexes PDFs downloaded for the diluted steganography literature review.

1. **Frustratingly Easy Edit-based Linguistic Steganography with a Masked Language Model**
   - File: `papers/2104.09833_ueoka_masked_lm_steganography.pdf`
   - Authors: Honai Ueoka, Yugo Murawaki, Sadao Kurohashi
   - Year: 2021
   - Source: NAACL 2021
   - Why relevant: Strong edit-based baseline; useful for keyword-anchored and localized hiding schemes.

2. **Provably Secure Generative Linguistic Steganography**
   - File: `papers/2021_findings_acl_provably_secure_generative_linguistic_steganography.pdf`
   - Authors: Siyu Zhang, Zhongliang Yang, Jinshuai Yang, Yongfeng Huang
   - Year: 2021
   - Source: Findings of ACL 2021
   - Why relevant: Formal security baseline for generative text steganography; useful comparison point for imperceptibility.

3. **Exploiting Language Model for Efficient Linguistic Steganalysis**
   - File: `papers/2107.12168_exploiting_language_model_for_efficient_linguistic_steganalysis.pdf`
   - Authors: Biao Yi, Hanzhou Wu, Guorui Feng, Xinpeng Zhang
   - Year: 2021
   - Source: arXiv
   - Why relevant: Detection baseline focused on steganalysis rather than generation.

4. **Hiding in Plain Sight: Towards the Science of Linguistic Steganography**
   - File: `papers/2312.16840_hiding_in_plain_sight_linguistic_steganography.pdf`
   - Authors: Leela Raj-Sankar, S. Raj Rajagopalan
   - Year: 2023
   - Source: arXiv
   - Why relevant: Conceptual framing around decodability, density, and detectability; useful for defining diluted-signal experiments.

5. **Zero-shot Generative Linguistic Steganography**
   - File: `papers/2403.10856_zero_shot_generative_linguistic_steganography.pdf`
   - Authors: Ke Lin, Yiyang Luo, Zijian Zhang, Ping Luo
   - Year: 2024
   - Source: NAACL 2024
   - Why relevant: Modern zero-shot LLM-based generation baseline with explicit imperceptibility evaluation.

6. **Generative Text Steganography with Large Language Model**
   - File: `papers/2404.10229_generative_text_steganography_with_llm.pdf`
   - Authors: Jiaxuan Wu, Zhengxian Wu, Yiming Xue, Juan Wen, Wanli Peng
   - Year: 2024
   - Source: ACM MM 2024
   - Why relevant: Black-box GPT-4/UI-based hiding method using keyword sets; especially relevant to dispersed trigger/keyword encodings.

7. **Towards Next-Generation Steganalysis: LLMs Unleash the Power of Detecting Steganography**
   - File: `papers/2405.09090_llms_unleash_power_detecting_steganography.pdf`
   - Authors: Minhao Bai, Jinshuai Yang, Kaiyi Pang, Huili Wang, Yongfeng Huang
   - Year: 2024
   - Source: arXiv
   - Why relevant: Direct evidence that LLMs can be used as stronger steganalysis models than older neural classifiers.

8. **Robust Steganography from Large Language Models**
   - File: `papers/2504.08977_robust_steganography_from_llms.pdf`
   - Authors: Neil Perry, Sanket Gupte, Nishant Pitta, Lior Rotem
   - Year: 2025
   - Source: arXiv
   - Why relevant: Robustness to paraphrasing and rerandomization; relevant to whether diluted signals survive surface edits.

9. **The Steganographic Potentials of Language Models**
   - File: `papers/2505.03439_steganographic_potentials_of_language_models.pdf`
   - Authors: Artem Karpov, Tinuade Adeleke, Seong Hah Cho, Natalia Perez-Campanero
   - Year: 2025
   - Source: ICLR 2025 workshop
   - Why relevant: Safety-oriented evaluation of covert message passing and hidden reasoning in frontier models.

10. **Early Signs of Steganographic Capabilities in Frontier LLMs**
    - File: `papers/2507.02737_early_signs_steganographic_capabilities_frontier_llms.pdf`
    - Authors: Artur Zolkowski, Kei Nishimura-Gasparian, Robert McCarthy, Roland S. Zimmermann, David Lindner
    - Year: 2025
    - Source: arXiv
    - Why relevant: Closest match to the hypothesis; evaluates whether current frontier LLMs can encode/decode messages and hidden reasoning.
